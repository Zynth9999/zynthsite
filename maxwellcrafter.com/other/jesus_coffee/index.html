Objective:

Find out if Jesus could be efficiently converted into a cup of coffee


Hypothesis:

A coffee made from Jesus would not be very good


Reason for this study:

It would be highly beneficial to society to have this knowledge


Definitions:

Google: The web search engine located at https://google.com
Coffee: A brewed drink, as defined by ISO 3509:2005


Steps:

Step 1: Find the height of Jesus
Step 2: Find the average mass of someone who is the same height of Jesus
Step 3: Find the percentage by mass of the amount of water in an average human
Step 4: Find how much mass a dehydrated Jesus would have
Step 5: Find the average mass of a communion wafer
Step 6: Find the amount of communion wafers contained in an average Jesus
Step 7: Find how much blood Jesus had
Step 8: Find the density of blood
Step 9: Find the mass of all the blood contained in a Jesus
Step 10: Find the density of wine
Step 11: Find the ratio of blood to wine (if Jesus turned his own blood to wine)
Step 12: Find the amount of wine that could be created out of 1 Jesus
Step 13: Find the ratio of the mass of communion wafers extracted from Jesus to the amount of wine extracted from Jesus
Step 14: Find the optimal amount of coffee grounds to water for making coffee
Step 15: Find out if the resulting ratio is good for making coffee


Research:

1. According to Google, Jesus was about 1.65m tall
2. The average weight of someone who is the same height of Jesus is around 63kg
3. The average human is about 60% water by mass
4. This means that a dehydrated Jesus would have a mass of around 25.2kg, which we can round to 25kg to simplify and account for previous rounding errors
5. According to Google average communion wafer has a mass of around 0.25g
6. 25kg divided by 0.25g equals 100,000, which would be the amount of communion wafers in the average Jesus
7. The average person who is the same height of Jesus has around 4.5L of blood
8. The density of blood is around 1.0565kg per liter
9. This means that Jesus most likely had around 4.75425kg of blood (before being dehydrated)
10. The density of wine is generally around 1.085kg per liter
11. 1.0565kg/1.085kg (the density of blood divided by the density of wine) gives 0.97373271889, the ratio of blood to wine when converted if following conservation of mass
12. The amount of wine created from a single Jesus would be the amount to begin with multiplied by the ratio of blood to wine, giving a result of 4.38179723501L, or 4.62936877878kg
13. The ratio of communion wafers to wine would be 25kg:4.62936877878kg, which can be simplified to roughly 5.4kg:1kg
14. According to Google, the recommended ratio of coffee grounds to water is about 1kg:17kg
15. Comparing these ratios, it seems that there would be about 91.8 times the amount of needed coffee grounds (communion wafers) than there should be for the coffee


Results:

The coffee would be much too strong, and the constistancy would most likely be closer to that of soup than the desired drink.


Conclusion:

A coffee made from Jesus would not work out very well, and would not at all be beneficial to society if it were created.
